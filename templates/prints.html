My 3D Prints

This page highlights some of my prints, their design parameters, and other in progrss design.


Centered Tables
Webcam Mount
This is a mount I designed to hold the webcam I use to monitor my 3D printer. It was designed to clasp onto the side of the printer. By using a dial caliper, it has very little wiggle room and is able to stay upright and stable with the web cam attached.
Weather Bowl
A product of my Design I Engineering class, the Weather Bowl is designed to determine the time of day that has the most ideal conditions for an outdoor run. To do this, I designed and 3D-printed an enclosed sensor network that would measure temperature, relative humidity, and light and placed it outdoors on campus. Each day, the sensor network collected data every thirty minutes and wirelessly published it to the Stevens MQTT server, allowing us to analyze how conditions were changing throughout the day.
Zumo Chassis
A product of my Design II Engineering class, the goal of this project is to design and configure a robot that is able to perform a series of tasks by going to selected coordinate points within an arena without colliding into obstacles. The 3D design of the Zumo Chassis robot was designed to be practical and have an even weight distribution in order for it to properly maneuver without tipping over
